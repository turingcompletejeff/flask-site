From a young age, I have always been interested in taking things apart and putting them back together. When I was first introduced to computers, I saw a universe of possibilites.

Throughout high school, I pursued networking, finding the endless complexity at every layer of computing.

After high school, I went to Missouri University of Science & Technology, where I dove into electronics, engineering, and of course computer science. Here, I learned to build robust algorithms and the hardware with which to run it. I joined the local chapter of ACM, serving as its treasurer, and joined a cyber security club. In both of these groups, I collaborated with other students to run practical workshops and facilitated presentations from guest speakers on campus.

After graduating, I put my algorithms & software engineering skills to the test at O'Reilly Auto. For the majority of my career here, I supported internal business analysts by designing new web apps and maintaining existing ones. Many of the apps I built here used SQL to conquer large, constant streams of part data, bringing them to analysts's fingertips in simple workflows and dashboards.
